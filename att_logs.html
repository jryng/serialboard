Calls made from Adnan’s cell phone on January 12 and 13th, 1999. Above the logs, a snippet from the fax cover sheet AT&T supplied alongside the original logs reads: “Outgoing calls only are reliable for location status. Any incoming calls will NOT be considered reliable information for location.”
   
   This warning was ignored and the information was used against Adnan anyway, until late 2015, when Abraham Waranowitz (back in 1999, an AT&T engineer; he testified at trial regarding the data AT&T handed over to the detectives) wrote an affidavit, in which he admits to not having known about the disclaimer.
   
   Despite this, the phone records are still factual information, and can be used to verify details in Jay and Adnan’s stories.
